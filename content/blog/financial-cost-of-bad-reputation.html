Quantifying Reputation Damage

A bad online reputation isn't just an embarrassment; it's a measurable financial liability. In 2026, consumers rely almost entirely on digital first impressions. Our recent study analyzed over 500 businesses across various sectors to determine the actual cost of negative search results.

The 22% Rule

We found that a single negative article appearing on the first page of Google search results for a brand name can lead to an average revenue loss of 22%. If there are three negative articles, that number jumps to nearly 60%.

  • Lost Talent: 74% of job seekers say they would not accept a job offer from a company with a bad reputation.
  • Lost Partnerships: B2B companies report a 40% higher friction rate in closing deals when negative press is visible.
  • Increased CAC: Customer Acquisition Costs rise by an average of 15% as companies have to spend more on advertising to overcome trust deficits.

The ROI of Reputation Management

Investing in reputation management—whether through suppression campaigns or legal removal—almost always yields a positive ROI within the first 6-12 months. Repairing a brand is no longer an optional PR exercise; it's a fundamental requirement for financial survival.
